        <h2 class="is-size-3">Every division and duration</h2>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">The everydiv eurorack is a playable polytemporal hardware instrument for the studio and the stage. It runs 6 independent clocks, each with its own division/duration pair — creating polyrhythms, polymeters, and tempo modulations that stay musically connected.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Instead of setting each clock to a fixed tempo, you set tuplet ratios. 15 divisions over 16 durations slows the clock to 112.5 BPM at a base of 120 — but the two clocks still realign every cycle. The ratios keep everything coherent, even when tempos diverge.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Two years in development with <a href="https://baem.studio/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Nicolas Bougaïeff</a> and <a href="/products/everydiv/team/">Jelle Akkerman</a>, the everydiv eurorack opens up rhythmic territory that simply isn't accessible with a single master clock.</p>

      <div class="column is-6">
        <p class="is-size-7 has-text-grey has-text-weight-bold mb-2">/ 02</p>
        <h2 class="is-size-3">Tuplets — the heart of it</h2>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">A tuplet is a ratio between a duration of time and the number of divisions within it. Change the divisions — squeeze in extra steps or stretch to fewer. Change the duration — move the point where clocks re-synchronise. Change both — and you're in new territory.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Divisions and durations range from 1 to 32, giving you 1024 possible ratios per clock — and six clocks running simultaneously. Pending changes are queued and take effect at the next cycle boundary, so everything stays tight during a performance.</p>

</section>

        <h2 class="is-size-3">Played on a Launchpad</h2>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Connect a Novation Launchpad via the front-panel USB host port. The top half of the grid sets divisions, the bottom half sets durations. Green shows the active value, amber a pending change, bright green tracks the playback position.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Select any of the 6 clocks from the top row. Each clock has its own settings page — clock type, output PPQN (2–96), reset mode, and duration sync mode. The everydiv eurorack supports Launchpad S, Mini (MK1–3), MK2, Pro (MK1, MK3), and X.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Full MIDI CC control of all parameters. Automate tuplet changes, tempo, and transport from your DAW or a hardware controller.</p>

<section class="section">

        <h2 class="is-size-3">Polytemporal recipes</h2>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4"><strong>Polytechno</strong> — Run hi-hats at 17/16 against a 16/16 kick. The hi-hats drift ahead and realign every bar, creating a constantly evolving groove.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4"><strong>Tempo portal</strong> — At 140 BPM, set a clock to 17/14 to produce exactly 170 BPM. Run a breakbeat sequencer at drum & bass speed while your main loop stays at techno tempo. Both clocks realign every 14 steps.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4"><strong>Ratchets</strong> — Feed two clock outputs into one sequencer via a mixer. One clock provides the base rhythm, the other adds bursts at a different rate.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4"><strong>Polytemporal sidechaining</strong> — Clock a sidechain compressor at 15/16 against a 16/16 kick. The pumping effect phases in and out of sync, adding movement to the mix.</p>
