The automat toolkit MK2 is the next generation of the [automat toolkit](/products/automat-toolkit/). Same plug & play philosophy — connect your favorite MIDI gear and trigger solenoids, motors, and actuators. But the MK2 adds a major new capability: **USB Host mode with a built-in step sequencer**.

### What's new in MK2

**USB Host mode** — Plug a Novation Launchpad directly into the automat's USB-A port. No computer needed. The automat powers the Launchpad and runs a full step sequencer on its grid. Supports Launchpad S, Mini (Mk1–3), Mk2, X, Pro, and Pro Mk3.

**Built-in sequencer** — 12 channels × 8 steps with per-step beats, accents, ratchets (×2–×4), skip steps (omit or shorten), per-channel length (1–8 for polymetric patterns), per-channel direction (forward, reverse, ping-pong, random), and Elektron-style trig conditions (1:2, 1:3, 1:4, 1:8). Tap tempo, nudge ±, and external MIDI clock sync.

**Backward compatible** — In Device mode, the MK2 appears as the same `dadamachines automat` USB MIDI device. Works with the same [WebUI configurator](https://config.dadamachines.com/), same learn modes, same accessories. Everything from the original toolkit carries over.

The MK2 includes the full accessory kit: 12 solenoids, mounting hardware, cables, and power supply.
