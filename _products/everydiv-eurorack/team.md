        <h2 class="is-size-3">Where it started</h2>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">The everydiv eurorack grew out of a shared conviction: electronic music's relationship with time is far too simple. Most instruments give you one clock, one tempo, one grid. But music has always been polytemporal — from West African polyrhythm to Ligeti's micropolyphony to the phasing of Steve Reich. The tools just haven't caught up.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Nicolas Bougaïeff brought the theory. His research into polytemporality and tuplet-based tempo modulation — what he calls <a href="http://pivotmixing.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pivot Mixing</a> — laid the musical foundation. Jelle Akkerman brought the engineering. Johannes Lohbihler at <a href="/about/">dadamachines</a> brought the product vision: a hands-on instrument that puts these ideas into a musician's rack, not a research paper.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Two years of development. The result is the everydiv — a eurorack module that makes polytemporal music playable, performable, and immediate.</p>

    <h2 class="is-size-3 mb-6">The team</h2>

    <!-- Nicolas Bougaïeff -->
    <div class="columns mt-5">
      <div class="column is-8">
        <h3 class="is-size-4 has-text-weight-bold">Nicolas Bougaïeff</h3>
        <p class="is-size-6 has-text-grey mb-3">Musical Concept & Research — <a href="https://baem.studio/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">BAEM</a> · <a href="https://novamute.co/artists/nicolas-bougaieff" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NovaMute</a></p>
        <p class="is-size-5">Nicolas is a Canadian-born, Berlin-based artist and researcher whose work bridges electronic dance music practice and musicological inquiry. He holds a PhD from the University of Huddersfield, where he was a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Research Fellow. He releases on Mute and NovaMute, and has performed at Berghain, fabric, and MUTEK.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">His current practice centres on polytemporality — the systematic use of tempo modulation, polyrhythmic structures, and tuplets within electronic dance music. He developed <a href="http://pivotmixing.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pivot Mixing</a>, a technique that challenges traditional understandings of temporality in electronic music. He is a contributing author to <em>The Cambridge Companion to Electronic Dance Music</em> (Cambridge University Press, 2026).</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">Earlier work includes co-founding Liine with Richie Hawtin and designing the controllers for Plastikman Live. The Lemur app he helped develop was used aboard the International Space Station. He founded the <a href="https://baem.studio/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Berlin Academy of Electronic Music</a>, where notable students include Charlotte de Witte.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <hr style="border-top: 1px solid #eee; margin: 2rem 0;">

    <!-- Jelle Akkerman -->
    <div class="columns">
      <div class="column is-8">
        <h3 class="is-size-4 has-text-weight-bold">Jelle Akkerman</h3>
        <p class="is-size-6 has-text-grey mb-3">Hardware & Firmware Engineering</p>
        <p class="is-size-5">Jelle designed and built the everydiv eurorack hardware and firmware — from the RP2350-based clock engine and USB host stack to the Launchpad grid interface and the SD card preset system. He translated Nicolas's polytemporal theory into precise, jitter-free clock outputs that stay musically coherent across all six channels.</p>
        <p class="is-size-5 mt-4">His firmware handles the real-time constraints of polytemporal clocking: queuing pending tuplet changes to cycle boundaries, maintaining phase-locked sync across clocks, and driving the Launchpad's visual feedback — green for active values, amber for pending changes, bright green tracking the playback position.</p>
      </div>

Seventh generation carpenter from Bavaria, raised in the Weilheim music scene alongside Lali Puna and The Notwist. DJ turned creator, bridging analog craft and electronic music.</p>
